Case Study — Manila, Philippines

De La Salle University (DLSU)

De La Salle University is a private research university located in Manila, Philippines, serving over 20,000 students across a dense, multi-building urban campus. As a large mixed-use academic institution operating year-round in a tropical climate with high cooling demands, it represents the exact conditions where ghost consumption and unmanaged energy waste become a severe and compounding financial burden.

The financial case, by the numbers

Annual electricity expenditure $2.79M USD ₱160.9 Million De La Salle University's estimated annual electricity cost based on historical consumption data from the DLSU Sustainability Report. This establishes the financial baseline the Digital Twin system is designed to reduce.
Energy wasted globally 30% Industry-wide average The average share of energy wasted by commercial and institutional buildings worldwide due to unmanaged occupancy inefficiencies, according to the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency. This figure applies universally — not just in the Philippines.
Projected annual savings $418K USD ₱24.1 Million Estimated yearly savings achievable at a conservative 15% reduction in consumption. Funds recovered can be redirected toward academic programs, research, and campus development instead of wasted electricity.
Return on investment per room < 2 Months ~$11 USD per sensor node Each smart node — consisting of a microcontroller, motion sensor, and temperature sensor — costs approximately ₱650 or $11 USD to deploy. At this price point, the system pays for itself within two billing cycles, making it viable for universities in both developing and developed economies.
Monthly cost reduction Up to 20% Per billing cycle Projected reduction in monthly electricity costs when occupancy-driven automated control replaces manual or schedule-based energy management. Applicable to any institution operating similar mixed-use spaces.
